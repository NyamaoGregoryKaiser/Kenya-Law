KENYA DEFENCE FORCES | NORTHEASTERN BORDER INTEL BRIEF
Classification: CONFIDENTIAL
Area: Garissa–Wajir corridor, Border Sector NE
DTG: 2025-09-17 / 0800EAT

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
- Border crossings generally stable; low-level smuggling likely persists along off-road tracks.
- No fresh indicators of organized cross-border incursions in last 72 hours.

THREATS
- Illicit trade networks leveraging seasonal livestock movement.
- Potential exploitation of unmanned tracks during night hours.
- Cyber/online messaging amplifying rumors of checkpoints being unmanned (unverified).

FRIENDLY DISPOSITION
- Multi-agency patrols along primary checkpoints; UAV reconnaissance allocated on-call.
- Community liaison ongoing with chiefs and elders for early warning.

ROUTES/KEY TERRAIN
- Dertu–Wajir route clear; sporadic washouts reported after rainfall.
- Key terrain: border riverine thickets, culverts, and dry riverbeds enabling concealment.

INDICATORS & WARNINGS
- Spike in fuel/vehicle part thefts within 30km of main checkpoints may indicate prepping for longer off-road runs.
- Unusual radio chatter on consumer bands near settlements.

RECOMMENDATIONS
- Increase random checkpoint timing and vary patrol routes.
- Night-time drone sweeps focused on culverts/dry riverbeds.
- Coordinate with customs for targeted inspections on fuel tankers and spare-part transporters.

Prepared by: NE Sector Intelligence Desk
